Frequently Asked Questions

1. What is Gitic

Answer: Gitic is a decentralized governance project that aims to provide an open and neutral platform for the governance of various blockchain networks. It introduces innovative concepts such as cross-chain interoperability states concept, smart contract constitution, Citizenship, yearly budget, and a decentralized decision-making process.

2. How does Gitic achieve cross-chain interoperability?

Answer: Gitic achieves cross-chain interoperability by implementing states bridges and smart contracts that facilitate the seamless movement of its native token (GITIC) between different blockchain networks. This ensures users can transact and participate in governance activities across supported blockchains.

3. What is the Smart Contract Constitution in Gitic

Answer: The Smart Contract Constitution in Gitic refers to a novel concept where the governance activities are executed through decentralized smart contract execution. This ensures transparency, immutability, and accessibility to all participants in the Gitic ecosystem.

4. How can I become a citizen of Gitic?

Answer: To become a citizen of Gitic, you need to be a member of one of the Gitic states (blockchain networks). Citizenship is typically granted to state members, and citizens have the right to vote, propose changes, and participate in Gitic's decentralized decision-making processes.

5. What are Gitic states?

Answer: Gitic states are various blockchain networks integrated into the Gitic decentralized governance system. Each state represents a distinct blockchain platform such as Ethereum, Solana, Cardano, and more. Gitic aims to unite users and citizens from different states under a common governance framework.

6. How is the yearly budget allocated in Gitic?

Answer: The yearly budget in Gitic is allocated to different fields such as space technology, software technology, innovation grants, investment funding, and more. This new budgeting concept replaces traditional token roadmaps and is designed to attract investors and foster innovation within the Gitic ecosystem.

7. Can I propose changes to the Gitic governance system?

Answer: Yes, as a citizen of Gitic, you have the right to propose changes to the governance system. Proposals can cover various aspects, including amendments to the Smart Contract Constitution, changes in budget allocations, and improvements to the overall Gitic ecosystem.

8. How is governance decision-making handled in Gitic?

Answer: Governance decision-making in Gitic is open to all citizens. Only citizens have the right to vote and participate in Gitic activities. Additionally, state members can vote within their state using the state's native token, but to vote on Gitic governance, they must use both their state native token and the Gitic governance token.

9. What is the Gitic implementation roadmap?

Answer: The Gitic implementation roadmap includes phases such as idea arrangement (2022), developing Gitic codes (2023), and launching on the testnet and presale (2024). The roadmap outlines the gradual development and deployment of Gitic's features and functionalities.

10. How can I get involved in Gitic partnerships and collaborations?

Answer: Partnerships and collaborations in Gitic are facilitated through proposals and voting by citizens. The community actively participates in deciding which partnerships align with Gitic's goals and values. Proposals for collaborations are subject to the democratic decision-making process within the Gitic governance system.
